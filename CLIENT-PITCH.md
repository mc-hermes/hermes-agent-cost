# gbrain Dashboard — Client Pitch & Selling Points

> A living map of everything your company knows. One URL. Zero maintenance. Coffee-money pricing.

---

## The One-Sentence Pitch

"We set up a private Wikipedia for your company that an AI keeps organized, and give you a dashboard that shows you — visually — how everything you know connects. It updates itself. You open it before any call and you're the most prepared person in the room."

---

## What They Actually Get

### 🧠 Company Memory That Doesn't Walk Out the Door

Every meeting note, client detail, project spec, and piece of research lives in one place. When someone leaves, their knowledge stays. New hires onboard by browsing the brain — it's the company's institutional memory, not someone's dusty Notion folder.

### 👤 Client Context Before Every Call

Before a sales call, open the dashboard. Click the prospect's name. You see: their role, company, email, phone, what you discussed last time, what stage they're at, what they're building, what motivates them, and what's blocking the deal. Everything from every past interaction. In one click.

### 🗺️ Visual Map of How Everything Connects

The knowledge graph shows all your pages as a living network — people connected to companies, meetings linked to projects, concepts referencing each other. Zoom in, drag things around, click to drill down. It's Wikipedia-style browsing for your own brain.

### 🩺 Health Dashboard — Is Your Knowledge Base Working?

50+ automated checks: Are all pages searchable? Are embeddings up to date? Are there orphan pages nobody links to? Green means healthy. Yellow means pay attention. Red means fix this. The brain monitors itself so you don't have to.

### 🔍 Search By Meaning, Not Keywords

Type "client who runs an education platform" and it finds TJ van der Molen at Passend — even if the word "education" appears as "learning platform" or "counselor training" in the notes. AI-powered semantic search, not keyword matching.

### 📊 Live Dashboard — Any Device, Any Time

One URL. Works on your laptop, your phone, your client's iPad during a meeting. Dark mode, light mode, Catppuccin, Tokyo Night — whatever you like. No app to install. No login to remember. It's just a web page.

## Objection Handling

### "We already use Notion/Confluence/SharePoint."

"Great — keep using it for documents. The brain doesn't replace where you write. It's where you go to *find and connect* what everyone has written. Think of it as the index, the map, and the search engine for all your existing tools."

### "This sounds expensive to maintain."

"The AI costs less than a dollar a year for a thousand pages. The dashboard is free to host. The VPS is optional. The only real cost is setup — and we can do a pilot with 20 pages for [your price] so you can see if it's worth it before committing."

### "We're too small for this."

"Small companies lose more from forgotten knowledge than large ones. When you're 5 people and one person leaves, that's 20% of your company's memory walking out the door. The brain is *most* valuable when you're small — it's your unfair advantage as you grow."

### "Is my data safe?"

"Everything lives on your own server or laptop. No third-party cloud. No vendor lock-in. The brain data is just markdown files — you can export them, back them up, or migrate them anytime. You own every byte."

### "I don't have time to maintain this."

"That's the point — you don't maintain it. Your team keeps taking notes the way they always have. The brain ingests them automatically. The dashboard refreshes itself. The health checks run on their own. It's a system that maintains itself."

## The Demo Script (5 Minutes)

1. **Open the dashboard.** "This is your company's brain. 18 pages right now — after a month, it'll be hundreds."
2. **Point at the knowledge graph.** "Every dot is a page. Every line is a connection the AI found automatically. Watch — I can zoom, drag, click." *Drag a node.*
3. **Click a person.** "Here's TJ van der Molen. Email, phone, company, what he's building, what we discussed, what's blocking the deal. Everything from every interaction. One click."
4. **Click a company.** "Passend. Three products in their pipeline. Stage. Contact. Next steps. This was populated from a single meeting note."
5. **Show the health panel.** "50 checks. Green across the board. If something breaks, we know before you do."
6. **Switch themes.** "Dark mode, light mode, Catppuccin — it's your brand. We can match your colours in 5 minutes."
7. **Close with the question:** "What if every person on your team had this open before every client interaction?"
