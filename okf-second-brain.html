The news

On June 12, 2026, Google published the Open Knowledge Format (OKF) — a vendor-neutral standard for AI agent knowledge. Plain markdown files. YAML frontmatter. No databases, no lock-in. If you can cat a file, you can read it. If you can git clone, you can ship it.

Karpathy predicted this. Google formalised it. The industry now agrees: agents need a knowledge format, and it's markdown.

Why this matters for your business

Right now your company knowledge lives in emails, Slack threads, meeting notes, invoices, and inside the heads of your 3 most senior people. When you ask an AI agent "what did we promise Client X last quarter?", it has to assemble the answer from 6 incompatible sources — or fail. OKF gives that knowledge a home. We make sure it actually gets there.
